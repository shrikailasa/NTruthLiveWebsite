Attempts to Burn the Supreme Pontiff of Hinduism and His Followers Alive

1. On 2nd March 2010 a locally popular politically-owned media house made a sensational and repeated screening of a fabricated video purporting to show The SPH with a well-known Indian film actress. The video was later proved to be false: A Judge ordered the television station to run an apology for it every two hours for 7 days, and the COO of the politically-owned media house confessed on public television as well as gave a sworn affidavit to a US court detailing the history and modus operandi of the blackmail and extortion and was jailed for the same[1]. This did not remedy the damage. The State suppressed these facts from appearing in the public discourse and also from appearing before the Indian judiciary[2]. The State-owned and controlled television channels within a few hours ensured the television telecast of the COO of the politically-owned media house was stopped; the ASMT has captured the video recording of the telecast and has this as evidence of the same.[3]

2. 3 March 2010, 9:00 AM 100 neo-Hindutva militant groups entered the Bengaluru monastery[4]. They ambushed the residence hut of the SPH. They pushed the SPH inside the hut and set it on fire[5]. The police were complicit in the crime. They prevented the monastery residents from putting down the fire and threatened the monastery residents of filing fabricated accusations against them such as possession of skin of endangered animals unless they forfeited the monastery[6]. To normalize[7] the society, politically-owned media houses and others news channels with neo-Hindutva affiliations aired the assassination attempt live. The SPH escaped from the bathroom window of the hut. The SPH continued to be hounded by the Police of three States[8], the neo-Hindutva media[9] , and State intelligence[10] for months. In Tiruvannamalai, Salem[11], (Bengaluru), Hosur, Pondicherry[12] attempts to mob lynch and kill the SPH occurred. The politically-owned media house which captured on camera the assassination attempt of the SPH by burning Him alive, followed Him over 3000 kilometers on road till Haridwar.

3. In March 2010, the local chief minister had given a press statement where he declared the State government’s intent to take control of private temples owned by the SPH and other Hindu spiritual leaders (Swamis)[13].

4. On 3 March 2010 the local chief minister made a public statement inciting hatred towards the SPH and the ASMT community. This was published in several newspapers[14] and cited by several media channels[15] (see full Tamil transcripts[16]).

a. The local chief minister stereotyped and vilified Swamis[17] (Hindu Monks) labeling them as “cheats (ஏமாற்றுவித்தைக்காரர்களையும்)”, “imposters (பகல்வேடக்காரர்களை)”, “brainwashers and destroyers of the life of the people (மக்களின் வாழ்வையும் அறிவையும் பாழாக்கி வருகின்ற)”, “swindlers of money (பணக் கொள்ளை அடிக்கின்ற)”, “despicable Swamis (கயமைத்தன சாமியார்களின்)”, “hoodwinkers who are disguising in the name of devotion”, “pushing the illiterate into a deep pit (இதுபோன்ற ஏமாற்றுவித்தைக்காரர்களையும், பக்தி வேடம் பூண்டு பாமர மக்களை படு குழியில் தள்ளுகின்ற பகல்வேடக்காரர்களையும்)”

b. The Chief Minister declared the stand of his government on Hindu monks (like the SPH), “my government cannot tolerate the despicable Swami’s hoodwinking acts (கயமைத்தன சாமியார்களின் ஏமாற்று வித்தைகளை பொறுத்துக் கொண்டிருக்க முடியாது).”

c. The Chief Minister described the practicing Hindus who followed Swamis (like the followers of the SPH) as, “People who are deeply buried in ignorance who cannot still understand these or even if understood do not want to change (அப்படியிருந்தும், இன்னமும் புரிந்துகொள்ள முடியாத, புரிந்துகொண்டாலும் திருந்திக் கொள்ள இயலாத மௌடீகத்தில் மூழ்கியோர் நாட்டில், சமூகத்தில் இருக்கத்தான் செய்கிறார்கள்)”, “those fickle-minded people who have fallen prey to these Swamis and destroying the society (இதுபோன்ற ஏமாற்றுவித்தைக்காரர்களையும், பக்தி வேடம் பூண்டு பாமர மக்களை படு குழியில் தள்ளுகின்ற பகல்வேடக்காரர்களையும், அவர்களிடம் பலியாகி சமுதாயத்தை சீரழிக்கின்ற சபல புத்தி உடையவர்களையும் இந்த அரசு வேடிக்கை பார்த்துக் கொண்டிருக்காது)”

d. The Chief Minister explained his stand on the practicing Hindus who followed Gurus like the SPH, “this government won’t be a mute spectator, of those fickle-minded people who have fallen prey to the Swamis and are destroying the society (அவர்களிடம் பலியாகி சமுதாயத்தை சீரழிக்கின்ற சபல புத்தி உடையவர்களையும் இந்த அரசு வேடிக்கை பார்த்துக் கொண்டிருக்காது)”

e. The Chief Minister pleaded to the citizens, “those who really want these preachers to be eradicated should stand by and support the actions my government is taking (ஊருக்கு உபதேசிகளை ஒழித்துக் கட்ட வேண்டுமென்று எண்ணுகின்ற ஒவ்வொருவரும் இந்த அரசு எடுக்கின்ற நடவடிக்கைகளுக்கு உறுதுணையாக இருக்க வேண்டும் என்று கேட்டுக் கொள்கிறேன்)”.

All they said was that they were late to arrive at the scene as their van had got punctured. The next day, 4 March 2010 media reported that no untoward incidents happened as the ASMT Ayurvedic Hospital and Temple had police protection[30]. In 2012 the Chief Operations Officer of the politically-owned media house gave a television interview accepting that the channel manufactured fake news to target the SPH[31]. Later in 2014, he reiterated this statement in an affidavit to a US court[32].
